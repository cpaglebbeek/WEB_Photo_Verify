1. Comparative Analysis vs. Industry Giants

While Adobe, Microsoft, and Google provide large-scale platform tools, PhotoVault addresses the specific needs of the individual sovereign creator.

Adobe: Content Authenticity Initiative (CAI / C2PA)

  • The Concept: Cryptographically signed manifests attached to image headers recording every edit.
  • PhotoVault Advantage: Adobe’s solution relies on metadata, which is easily stripped by social media platforms or screenshots. PhotoVault’s Chroma Stealth Stamp is embedded in the pixels themselves, surviving even when the header is destroyed.

Microsoft: PhotoDNA

  • The Concept: Robust hashing to identify known illegal content across platforms.
  • PhotoVault Advantage: PhotoDNA is a reactive tool for platforms to find content. PhotoVault is a proactive tool for creators to establish ownership before a dispute occurs, specifically solving the "Proof of Origin" problem.

Google: Reverse Image Search & Lens

  • The Concept: AI-driven visual similarity search across the web.
  • PhotoVault Advantage: Google provides "visual matches" but no mathematical proof. PhotoVault provides a Forensic Diff Map and Hamming Distance, delivering court-ready statistical evidence of content derivation.

2. Unique Concepts: The "Triple-Lock" Kit

No other tool currently combines these three distinct layers of evidence into a single, unified workflow:

  1. Digital Layer (Invisible Stamp): Pixel-level data storage that survives re-saves.
  2. Forensic Layer (Visual DNA): Content-level identification that handles cropping and scaling.
  3. Physical Layer (1-Pixel Border): A geometric "puzzle piece" proof that requires the owner to possess the exact original dimensions.
